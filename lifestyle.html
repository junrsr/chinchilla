What do I need?

How can you look after me?

A good diet.

The RSPCA suggests that I should have constant access to clean water and good quality hay. I naturally eat grasses, leaves and twigs, but hay will do! Futhermore, I should be given a small amount of grass-based chinchilla pellets daily to protect my teeth. I should only get dried fruits and root vegetables as treats but feel free to give them more frequently.










The correct environment.

The size and temperature of anywhere I'm kept needs to be appropriate, ideally in well ventilated, dry areas between 10-18ÂºC. I'd love to have large, secure living environemnts with plenty of space to jump about and run around, whilst also having a quiet space away from other pets due to my prey nature. I'd love to have some non-plastic toys to play with and obviously a tray filled with chinchilla dust so I can take a bath.

Building on this, a clean, safe, secure, hazard-free environment would really be ideal. This means regularly cleaning my cage, constant access to enough nest boxes to sleep and keep me warm, and a hiding place in case I feel afraid. At the end of the day, I just want to feel comfortable and safe.

















Welfare checks.

It might be hard to tell if I'm in pain. I'm not very good at showing outwards signs of this, so a lot of my suffering may go unnoticed. Because of this, you should check for illness daily, including looking for sore feet, wetness around eyes/mouth and fur loss. If so, contact a vet immediately. Changes in normal behaviour patters could also be a early sign of illness.
